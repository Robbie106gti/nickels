Category: Header Drop-Down Menu - Special Finish

Item Name: Drag Glaze


Application

  • Drag Glaze Colors:

Drag Beach

Drag Ebony

Drag Espresso

Drag Fawn

Drag Pearl


Specifications

Available Materials: All stained Wood and Painted materials.

Finishing:
  • STEP 1: Steps 1-8 of Stain Finishing Process or steps 1-6 of Paint Finishing Process.
  • STEP 2: The finished surface is scored with a 180 grit sandpaper in the direction of the grain.
  • STEP 3: A coating of glaze color is applied to the entire surface.
  • STEP 4: The surface is then wiped off and the glaze is left “hanging” in the scored area left by the sandpaper which gives the surface the characteristic “brush” strokes.
  • STEP 5: The top-coat is applied to all glazed surfaces.
  • STEP 6: Final inspection for defects and color.

Size: not applicable

Pricing: percent up-charge on all finished products.

Construction: see Finishing

Options: Drag-Beach, Drag-Ebony, Drag-Espresso, Drag-Fawn, Drag-Pearl, Drag-Custom.

Restrictions: not available in all Melamines or Engineered Veneer.

DRY GLAZE »

Category: Header Drop-Down Menu - Special Finish

Item Name: Dry Glaze


Application

  • Dry Glaze Colors:

Dry Clay

Dry Dune

Dry Dusk

Dry Midnight

Dry Snow


Specifications

Available Materials: All stained Wood and Painted materials.

Finishing:
  • STEP 1: Steps 1-8 of Stain Finishing Process or steps 1-6 of Paint Finishing Process.
  • STEP 2: A coating of glaze color is sprayed on to all finished surfaces and is left to set.
  • STEP 3: The entire surface is rubbed with a scotch-brite pad, leaving the glaze within the profiles and inside corners of the door, as well as small remnants of paint on the flat surfaces of the door creating an “air brush” finish.
  • STEP 4: The surface is then wiped off and the glaze is left “hanging” in the scored area left by the sandpaper which gives the surface the characteristic “brush” strokes.
  • STEP 5: The top-coat is applied to all glazed surfaces.
  • STEP 6: Final inspection for defects and color.

Size: not applicable

Pricing: percent up-charge on all finished products.

Construction: see Finishing

Options: Dry-Clay, Dry-Dune, Dry-Dusk, Dry-Midnight, Dry-Snow, Dry-Custom.

Restrictions: not available in all Melamines or Engineered Veneer.

A sample door with the dry glaze color is strongly recommended.

WIRE BRUSH ...

Category: Header Drop-Down Menu - Special Finish

Item Name: Wire Brush


Application

  • Wire Brush Process

Specifications

Available Materials: Available on PS White Oak, QS White Oak and RC White Oak.

Finishing:
  • STEP 1: Bare wood surfaces are inspected for defects. .
  • STEP 2: Use a hard-bristle wire brush to scrape out the loose grain portions of the material. This creates voids in the material that allow the glaze to hang up. Brushing is done in the same direction as the natural grain of the wood.
  • STEP 3: Material is conditioned (sanded) for staining which includes removal of defects.
  • STEP 4: Initial toner dye is applied to the surface to achieve the base color.
  • STEP 5: A wiping stain is sprayed on the surface, then immediately wiped to achieve final color.
  • STEP 6: Stained material is inspected for defects and color consistency.
  • STEP 7: Control sample offcut from the batch is included in the job for future reference.
  • STEP 8: Sealer coat is sprayed on all stained surfaces.
  • STEP 9: All sealed surfaces are hand-sanded and inspected.
  • STEP 10: All sanded surfaces are top-coated with a non-yellowing, low HAPS, clear conversion varnish.
  • STEP 11: Glaze is sprayed on to entire piece.
  • STEP 12: Excess material is removed. The glaze hangs up in all exposed crevasses.
  • STEP 13: The top coat is applied before the material is sent to the heat booth for drying.
  • STEP 14: Final inspection for defects and color.

Size: not applicable

Pricing: percent up-charge on all finished products.

Construction: see Finishing

Options: Wire Brush-Dune, Wire Brush-Midnight, Wire Brush-Snow.

Restrictions: not available in all Melamines or Engineered Veneers.

HERITAGE »

Category: Header Drop-Down Menu - Special Finish

Item Name: Heritage


Application

  • Heritage Process includes a combination of Worm Holes, Worn Corners & Edges, Dents & Dings and Rasping with a Dray Glaze added: applied PRIOR to staining or painting.

Specifications

Available Materials: only available in Wood doors.

Finishing:
  • Worm Holes - a random distribution of small hole patterns that run in the direction of the grain; applied prior to staining or painting for a subtle result.
  • Worn Corners & Edges - the outside corners of the frame and raised center panel of the door are sanded to a rounded edge and the leading edges of the door are sanded in random places; applied prior to staining or painting for a subtle result.
  • Dents & Dings - a random distribution of a variety of shaped and sized indentations into the wood; applied prior to staining or painting.
  • Rasping - the outside edges of the door frame are randomly marked with a rasp; applied prior to staining or painting.
  • Dry Glaze - Once the base color has been completed, a coating of glaze color is applied to the entire surface and is left to set. This entire surface is then rubbed with a scotch-brite pad, leaving the glaze within the profiles and inside corners of the door, as well as small remnants of paint on the flat surfaces of the door creating an “air brush” finish. There will be light scuffing on the flat surfaces due to the nature of this technique.

Size: not applicable

Pricing: percent up-charge on all finished products.

Construction: see Finishing

Options: Heritage-Aged-Dry-Clay, Heritage-Aged-Dry-Dune, Heritage-Aged-Dry-Dusk, Heritage-Aged-Dry-Midnight, Heritage-Aged-Dry-Snow, Heritage-Aged-Dry-Custom.

Restrictions: not available in MDF, all Melamines or Engineered Veneer.

Category: Header Drop-Down Menu - Special Finish

Item Name: CUSTOM


Application

  • CUSTOM

Specifications

Available Materials: only available in Wood doors.

Custom Stain Options

A sample of the stain to be matched must be provided to Nickels Finishing Team Leader (via the Dealer Service). This sample must have the customer name and dealer name attached as well as the wood species to be stained. Allow 4 - 5 working days for the Finishing Team to achieve a proper match and produce samples. Along with the original sample, 2 matched samples will be provided with the custom stain number and wood species labelled on the back. The Finishing Team retains a 5th matched sample for reference. Writen approval is required before confirming order.

A sample door with the final approved color is strongly recommended.

Sheens and other special finishes are not included in this process and must be noted individually on every order. There will be a setup fee charged every time a new stain color is requested using the Customization Charge: CC-STAIN. Send in a separate order 2 weeks prior to sending job to inbox with request for scustom color. The entire custom stain process can take up to 2 weeks to complete, so please plan accordingly. No order will be processed until a custom color has been approved and confirmed with a signature.

Custom Paint Option

The custom paint procedure will be similar to the custom stain procedure except in the case where the color requested matches an existing laminate or paint color that is available as a sample in Vancouver. In this case, samples will be made to match the color requested and given a custom number.

A sample door with the final approved color is strongly recommended.

Sheens and other special finishes are not included in this process and must be noted individually on every order. There will be a setup fee charged every time a new paint color is requested using the Customization Charge: CC-PAINT. The entire custom paint process can take up to 2 weeks to complete, so please plan accordingly. No order will be processed until a custom color has been approved and confirmed with a signature.

Custom Glaze Option:

The custom glaze procedure will be similar to the custom stain procedure except in the case where the color requested matches an existing laminate or paint color that is available as a sample in Vancouver. In this case, samples will be made to match the color requested and given a custom number.

A sample door with the final approved color is required.

Sheens and other special finishes are not included in this process and must be noted individually on every order. There will be a setup fee charged every time a new stain color is requested using the Customization Charge: CC-GLAZE. The entire custom stain process can take up to 2 weeks to complete, so please plan accordingly. No order will be processed until a custom color has been approved and confirmed with a signature.
