5/8 Alder »

Category: Header Drop-Down Menu - 5/8 Interior Mtrl

Item Name: Alder


Description

  • This is a straight-grained and evenly textured medium hardwood with a pleasing if not subdued figure. Color ranges from a pale pinkish/brown to a light tan and the wood accepts stain very consistently. Alder is an abundant and rapid growing tree, native to the Northwest region. Available with either Clear or Knotty grade. Clear Alder exhibits the presence of occasional small, sound knots and some mineral streaks. Knotty Alder is truly characterized by its knots which range in size and character from tight and sound to split and open with a random distribution.


Specifications

Finishing: Click on Paint Finishing Process to see how paint is applied. Click on Stain Finishing Process to see how stain is applied.

Size: Not applicable

Pricing: Parts and doors are calculated by Webquoin when Alder is chosen.

Construction: See finishing

Options:
  • Stains available: Sesame, Blue Corn, Chipotle, Coal, Coffee Bean, Cardamon, Ecru, Hazelnut, Oyster, Pebble, Polar, Shiitake, Sienna, Smoke, Tamarind and Thunder.
  • Paints available: Chantilly Lace, Chelsea Grey, Distance, Frosty White, Grey Owl, Hale Navy, Kendall Charcoal, Off The Grid, Quiet Time, Simply White, Shore White, White Dove and Black, and Custom.

Restrictions: Not applicable

Knotty Alder veneer is not available so Clear Alder veneer will be used when veneers are necessary, such as for center panels on a recessed panel door, finish panels, baseboards, etc.

Ordering Information

  • On the Interior Mtrl Header Drop-Down Menu in WQ, click on 5/8 Materials then 5/8 WOOD... and then choose Alder from the list.

5/8 Maple »

Category: Header Drop-Down Menu - 5/8 Interior Mtrl

Item Name: Maple


Description

  • This is a strong, fine textured hardwood with a uniform grain and very subtle figure. The color is creamy white with a slight reddish brown tinge. Maple will exhibit random black to grey mineral streaks in addition to areas of burl, birdseye, quilting, and worm tracks. These characteristics are considered normal and part of what gives Maple its unique appeal.
